How to be a Mentor

Being a mentor means being able to make yourself available for someone so that you can support them through any problems they have and also advise them from your experience. Being a mentor also means keeping your mentee’s priority in mind so they can improve. A good mentor should be genuinely interested and wanting to help their mentor. Lastly a mentor can come in all shapes and sizes so it can be anyone that you feel fills the description.

Being emotionally intelligent is a big part of being an amazing mentor. Any time you become a mentor for someone, you'll find yourself getting to know their unique personality, their wants and needs, the experience

The best mentors know how to unlock this information by asking the right questions, reading their mentee's body language, being open-minded, and even acknowledging and controlling their own emotions.

Leadership

To be a leader means to set an example for others of what it is to be a leader and to make them better leaders that you once were. Leadership is also a way to postivitley leave an impact on people's lives and teach them life values that will reside with them. Leadership is to promote the good things to do in life and to find yourself as an open person to give other comfortability

Positivity

To be a good mentor you promote positivity. Life is short. Why waste time on negative behaviors that don't align with your business' moral compass? I proactively encourage my team to think positively--All. The. Time. Even when things seem to be spinning out of control or we didn't achieve the result we anticipated, positive thinking will eventually cultivate positive outcomes. Setting weekly, monthly and yearly positive intentions as a group will help to align your team and ensure that everyone is facing toward the same North Star.

Motivation

To be a good mentor you motivate your mentee to push forward. Do these following things

  1. Limit the amount of time or effort that you’re asking for
  2. Share in the sacrifice
  3. Appeal to their emotions
  4. Give people multiple reasons for doing what you want them to do
  5. Be the change you want to inspire
  6. Appeal to people’s value system
  7. Tell a story
  8. Trust people
  9. Challenge them

Growth

To be a good mentor you promote a growth mindset. A growth mindset is when people know they can develop their skills and talents through effort and persistence, as well as being receptive to lessons and feedback. They generally believe they can improve through hard work and trying new learning methods. You can do these following things:

  1. Avoid Praising Intelligence and Sheer Effort
  2. Use Diverse Teaching Strategies
  3. Introduce Simple Gamification Elements
  4. Teach the Values of Challenges
  5. Explain the Purposes of Abstract Skills and Concepts
  6. Allow Time for Goal-Based Journaling
  7. Say “Yet” More Often
  8. Help People Change their Language
  9. Use Success Folders
